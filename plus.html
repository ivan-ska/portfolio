+Plus App

+Plus is a clean, universal counter app that does exactly what you expect. It’s designed to be simple, distraction-free, and flexible enough to fit a wide range of everyday tasks. No learning curve, no clutter, just tap and count.

The app is very friendly to adapt to your needs. Whether you are tracking things, people, habits or something else. Sometimes the best tool is the one you don’t notice.

The core idea behind +Plus is simple, clear, genuinely useful. While mapping the UX flow, I realized the app could be easily adapted to all kinds of counting and tracking needs: fitness, habit-building, inventory or more.

But each of these would require different tweaks and features. So instead of expanding in every direction, I chose to stay focused. That clarity is reflected in the interface: minimal, distraction-free, built to get you straight to the point. Like a pocket tool that just works.

When you launch the app for the first time, they’re not greeted with tutorials, pop-ups, or onboarding screens. Instead, you’re greeted with a clean open interface, that immediately invites to create first tracker card. Right on the home screen.

The process unfolds step by step, with each action feeling natural and self-explanatory. No confusion or clutter, just a quiet confidence that the app is there to help. By the final step you already see your new tracker taking shape. This helps to visualize the result and instantly return to previous steps to change something.

The main screen evolves naturally as the you add and update trackers. Each card feels like a small self-contained tool. To increase or decrease the number, simply tap the card and press a button. If needed, you can also enter a value manually. No hidden gestures or guesswork.

It’s easy to dive deeper. You can just tap a card and it smoothly slides up to reveal stats. Everything is designed to feel obvious without instructions. Needed to make a change? Editing a tracker takes through the same familiar steps as creating one, just with your data already in place.

Modern iOS lets you interact directly with widgets. A tracker card can live right on the home screen always ready for quick input. No need to open the app — just tap, update, and move on. All changes are instantly synced with the app and reflected in your stats.

Due to iOS limitations, the widget has a slightly different look and behavior, animations feel a bit more reserved. But that’s also what makes it clean and fast. You can even add multiple widgets at once for quick access to most important trackers.
