nuk Website

Visual identity, design system, and interface for a curated online marketplace featuring unique objects by makers, artists, and small brands. Each item is handpicked, making the collection feel personal and meaningful.

I created a visual style that brings out the brand’s character and used it to shape the entire site, from structure to details. It turned into a calm and distinctive space, much like the products it brings together.

Before designing the full site, I started with the elements that shape the experience: color, type, motion, and interaction.

Custom drawn by hand icons add a personal, crafted feel. The product cards are clean and calm, showing only what matters at first glance. More info appears on hover, keeping the layout light and focused. Special tags like "organic" or "unique" become subtle icons near product names. This helps visually distinguish products more easily and creates space for different combinations of card appearances.

Animations aren’t just for decoration here. They play a key role in how the site feels. Every motion is smooth and intentional, making the interface feel alive without ever getting in the way.

The site opens with a short animation. Not just to impress, but to invite. It gives a moment to shift gears, to step into a slower, more thoughtful space.

This isn’t about fast shopping or endless scrolling. The homepage gently guides you: a curated mix of product cards and featured stories, interviews with the makers themselves. Everything here is designed to slow you down just enough to notice. The layout, the rhythm, the silence between clicks — it all adds up to something that feels more like a personal corner than a commercial platform.

The filters in the Shop section adapt to your choices, helping you discover what truly resonates. Each product page is not just about specs. It gives context, links to other works by the creator, their story, interviews, and a personal profile. Everything is designed to help you not just buy something, but understand where it comes from, who made it, and why it matters.

This isn’t just a marketplace. It’s a space for thoughtful discovery, built around meaning and the people behind the objects.
